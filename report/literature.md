# Literature

Standing reference of paper summaries. One entry per cited PDF. Order: alphabetical by citation key.

## chenGYS1InducesGlycogen2020

Chen et al. examined 306 ccRCC patients and found GYS1 (glycogen synthase 1) and glycogen are significantly elevated in ccRCC versus normal kidney and correlate with worse overall survival and higher T stage / Fuhrman grade. GYS1 overexpression in Caki-1/Caki-2 cells increased glycogen content, proliferation, colony formation, and xenograft growth, while silencing did the opposite. Metabolomics and RNA-seq showed GYS1 silencing perturbed carbohydrate metabolism and downregulated NF-κB signaling; mechanistically GYS1 promoted p65 phosphorylation and nuclear translocation indirectly via the chaperone RPS27A, and p65 knockdown rescued the growth phenotype. GYS1 silencing also resensitized cells to sunitinib. The paper cites prior work that hypoxia rapidly induces GYS1 and that HIF1α/HIF2α regulate glycogen in VHL-deficient ccRCC, but does not itself show HIF → GYS1 regulation; its mechanistic focus is GYS1 → RPS27A → NF-κB-driven proliferation.

## craigFibrolamellarCarcinomaLiver1980

Craig and colleagues compiled clinical and pathologic data on 23 patients with a histologically distinctive variant of hepatocellular carcinoma, which they named fibrolamellar carcinoma of the liver. The variant predominated in patients aged 5–35 years, occurred equally in both sexes, and arose in non-cirrhotic livers. Two defining histologic features were identified: deeply eosinophilic neoplastic hepatocytes containing intracytoplasmic hyaline globules and pale bodies, and fibrosis arranged in a lamellar pattern around the tumor cells. Mean survival (32 months) and operability (48%) substantially exceeded those of ordinary HCC. The authors urged that this entity be recognized separately because it had been confused with focal nodular hyperplasia and hepatocellular adenoma.

## derksenSomaticInactivationEcadherin2006

Derksen and colleagues generated conditional Cdh1 mutant mice and crossed them with K14cre;Trp53F/F animals to test the in vivo role of E-cadherin loss in mammary tumorigenesis. Combined epithelial-specific inactivation of E-cadherin and p53 produced accelerated, multifocal mammary carcinomas histologically resembling human invasive lobular carcinoma, characterized by small uniform cells arranged in trabecular or "single-file" patterns; E-cadherin loss alone did not initiate tumors, and p53 loss alone produced non-lobular adenocarcinomas / solid carcinomas. Mechanistically, loss of E-cadherin conferred anoikis resistance and promoted angiogenesis, both contributing to metastatic spread. The authors conclude that E-cadherin loss contributes to both initiation of the lobular phenotype and to metastasis in this synergistic model with p53 loss.

## duHIFDrivesLipid2017

Du et al. demonstrated that lipid accumulation in ccRCC cell lines (RCC4, RCC10, 786-O) is VHL-dependent and occurs regardless of lipid source. They identified CPT1A, the rate-limiting mitochondrial fatty-acid import enzyme, as a direct HIF1- and HIF2-repressed target gene; VHL reintroduction raised CPT1A mRNA ~4-fold and hypoxia reversed this, while ChIP showed HIF binding at the CPT1A locus. CPT1A repression diverts fatty acids away from β-oxidation and into lipid droplets, and forced CPT1A re-expression in VHL-null cells reduced lipid droplets and limited xenograft tumor growth. In TCGA, lower CPT1A expression and activity tracked with ccRCC tumors and worse patient outcomes. The authors conclude that HIF-mediated CPT1A repression is essential for ccRCC lipid deposition and tumorigenesis.

## eischenDisruptionARFMdm21999

Eischen et al. tested whether the ARF–Mdm2–p53 tumor-suppressor axis restrains Myc-driven B-cell lymphomagenesis in vivo using Eμ-myc transgenic mice. In primary IL-7-dependent pre-B cell cultures, conditional Myc activation (Myc-ERTM) induced rapid p53-dependent apoptosis (>60% death within 12 h), whereas ARF-/-, p53-/-, and ARF/p53 double-null pre-B cells were markedly resistant; freshly explanted Eμ-myc bone marrow pre-B cells showed elevated p19ARF and high spontaneous apoptosis. In 25 spontaneously arising Eμ-myc lymphomas, 28% had p53 missense mutations or deletions and 24% had biallelic ARF deletions (mutually exclusive), with Mdm2 overexpressed in additional tumors — disabling the pathway in roughly half of cases. ARF+/− Eμ-myc mice developed lymphoma with an 11-week mean survival (vs ~30 weeks for ARF+/+), with 80% of tumors losing the remaining wild-type ARF allele; ARF-/- Eμ-myc mice died within ~7 weeks. Myc overexpression in B cells thus strongly selects in vivo for inactivation of the ARF–Mdm2–p53 apoptotic checkpoint.

## evanInductionApoptosisFibroblasts1992

Based on the published record: Evan et al. (Cell 1992, 69:119–128) showed that constitutive c-Myc expression in serum-deprived Rat-1 fibroblasts induced rapid apoptosis rather than quiescence; the N-terminal Myc domains required for cotransformation and transactivation were also required for apoptosis, demonstrating that proliferation and apoptosis are coupled, intrinsic consequences of Myc activation. This is the foundational paper establishing the dual-effector model of Myc — that Myc deregulation simultaneously drives proliferation and a latent apoptotic program, the resolution of which requires cooperating mutations that disable apoptosis. ## frixenEcadherinmediatedCellcellAdhesion1991

Frixen et al. tested whether E-cadherin expression governs the invasive behavior of human carcinoma cells across panels of bladder, breast, lung, and pancreas carcinoma lines. Using immunofluorescence and Western/Northern blotting, they showed that epithelioid, noninvasive lines uniformly expressed E-cadherin, whereas fibroblastoid, invasive lines had lost expression. Critically, transfecting E-cadherin cDNA into invasive E-cadherin-negative lines restored epithelial morphology and abolished invasion in collagen-gel and embryonal-heart assays, and this rescue was reversed by anti-E-cadherin monoclonal antibodies. The authors concluded that selective loss of E-cadherin drives dedifferentiation and invasion of carcinoma cells, identifying E-cadherin as an invasion suppressor. The paper does not specifically address invasive lobular carcinoma morphology.

## grignaniFusionProteinsRetinoic1998

Grignani et al. showed that both PML-RARα and PLZF-RARα APL fusion proteins recruit the N-CoR / histone deacetylase corepressor complex through the RARα CoR box, and that PLZF-RARα contains a second, RA-resistant N-CoR binding site in the PLZF N-terminal region. Physiological doses of retinoic acid (RA) dissociate N-CoR/HDAC from PML-RARα but not from PLZF-RARα, explaining the differential RA responsiveness of the two APL subtypes. Mutation of the N-CoR-binding site (AHT) abolished PML-RARα's ability to block vitamin D3 / TGF-β-induced myeloid differentiation of U937 cells, while the HDAC inhibitor trichostatin A restored RA-induced transglutaminase expression and differentiation in PLZF-RARα cells. Aberrant HDAC recruitment by APL fusion proteins is established as crucial to their transforming activity, locking target genes (and hence myeloid maturation) in a repressed chromatin state.

## honeymanDetectionRecurrentDNAJB1PRKACA2014

Honeyman et al. performed whole-transcriptome and whole-genome sequencing on paired tumor/adjacent-normal samples from FL-HCC patients and identified a single recurrent chimeric transcript fusing exon 1 of DNAJB1 to exons 2–10 of PRKACA (the PKA catalytic subunit α), produced by a ~400 kb deletion on chromosome 19. The fusion was detected in 15 of 15 FL-HCC patients (29 samples including primaries, metastases, recurrences) and was absent from all matched normal tissues. The chimeric protein was confirmed by immunoprecipitation and Western blot, and expression in cultured cells showed the fusion retains kinase activity. The authors conclude that the recurrent DNAJB1-PRKACA chimera likely contributes to FL-HCC pathogenesis. The paper does not test whether the fusion produces the lamellar fibrosis morphology.

## linRoleHistoneDeacetylase1998

Lin et al. independently showed that PML-RARα and PLZF-RARα associate in vivo with the histone deacetylase complex (SMRT / N-CoR, mSin3A, HDAC1), with PLZF-RARα binding HDAC machinery additionally through its BTB/POZ domain in a ligand-insensitive fashion. Gel-shift assays demonstrated that pharmacological RA dissociates SMRT from PML-RARα but requires substantially higher concentrations than for wild-type RARα, while an RA-resistant PML-RARα mutant (m4) from NB4-R4 cells cannot release SMRT at all. The HDAC inhibitors trichostatin A and sodium butyrate synergized with all-trans RA to overcome the dominant-negative block on RARE reporters and to potentiate (PML-RARα) or restore (PLZF-RARα, NB4-R4) RA-induced granulocytic differentiation of NB4 cells. The work establishes targeted HDAC recruitment by APL fusions as the mechanistic link between oncogenic RAR fusions and the differentiation block of APL, and identifies HDAC inhibition as a therapeutic strategy.

## merinoMorphologicSpectrumKidney2007

Merino et al. reviewed 40 renal tumors from 38 patients with HLRCC and confirmed germline FH mutations. Tumors were large (2.3–20 cm), almost always unilateral, and showed varied architecture (papillary, tubulopapillary, tubular, solid, mixed). The authors define a unifying hallmark: a characteristic large nucleus with a very prominent inclusion-like orangiophilic / eosinophilic nucleolus surrounded by a clear halo. Immunohistochemistry was non-specific, but LOH at 1q32 and 1q42-44 was frequent. The tumors behaved aggressively with frequent regional lymph-node spread, and the authors conclude that morphologic recognition by the pathologist is currently the best diagnostic tool.

## pescadorHypoxiaPromotesGlycogen2010

Pescador et al. identified GYS1 (muscle glycogen synthase) as a novel HIF-target gene via bioinformatic search for conserved hypoxia response elements. In C2C12 myotubes, primary hepatocytes, and other cell lines, hypoxia (1% O₂) strongly induced GYS1 mRNA and protein in an actinomycin-D-sensitive (transcriptional) manner; HIF1α siRNA and HIF-deficient HepaC4 cells abrogated this induction, and ChIP plus luciferase reporter assays confirmed direct HIF1α binding to a functional HRE at -314 in the GYS1 promoter (mutation abolished hypoxic induction). Hypoxia also increased glycogen synthase activity, UGP2 and GBE1 expression, and total glycogen accumulation; GYS1 or HIF1α knockdown blunted glycogen build-up while GYS1 overexpression mimicked it. GYS1 silencing impaired hypoxic preconditioning, suggesting the stored glycogen supports anaerobic ATP supply during sustained hypoxia. Experiments are in non-renal cells; the paper establishes the general HIF / GYS1 / glycogen mechanism, not ccRCC-specific evidence.

## qiuHIF2aDependentLipidStorage2015

Qiu et al. analyzed ccRCC patient tumors and TCGA RNA-seq data, finding that PLIN2, a lipid droplet coat protein, is overexpressed in ccRCC and positively correlated with HIF2α (but not HIF1α) activation, with HIF1α silencing actually increasing PLIN2 levels. In 786-O and RCC4 ccRCC cell lines, HIF2A knockdown reduced PLIN2 mRNA and protein, and PLIN2 was required for lipid droplet accumulation, cell proliferation, and xenograft tumor viability. Mechanistically, PLIN2-dependent lipid storage maintained endoplasmic reticulum integrity and suppressed cytotoxic ER stress (UPR / PERK / IRE1α) responses provoked by ccRCC's high protein synthetic load and nutrient/oxygen limitation. The HIF2α–PLIN2–lipid-droplet axis underlies the "clear-cell" phenotype, and ER stress is identified as a targetable vulnerability when this axis is suppressed.

## schmitzBurkittLymphomaPathogenesis2012

Schmitz et al. combined RNA-seq of 28 sporadic Burkitt lymphoma (BL) biopsies + 13 BL lines with RNA-interference screens to define genetic and functional drivers of BL beyond the defining MYC translocation. They found that ~70% of sporadic BL cases carry mutations in TCF3 (E2A) or its negative regulator ID3 — gain-of-function TCF3 mutations and loss-of-function ID3 mutations both increase TCF3 activity, on which all BL lines depend. Active TCF3 sustains tonic B-cell receptor signaling and PI3K / AKT / mTOR activation (BL lines were sensitive to BKM120 and rapamycin), partly by repressing SHP-1 (PTPN6) and upregulating BCR components. Additionally, 38% of sporadic BL (and 67% of HIV-associated, but only 1.8% of endemic) cases harbor CCND3 mutations producing stabilized cyclin D3 isoforms that accelerate G1/S transit; BL lines and xenografts were highly sensitive to the CDK4/6 inhibitor PD 0332991. The work establishes BL as pathogenetically distinct from DLBCL and identifies TCF3 / ID3, tonic BCR / PI3K, and CCND3-CDK6 as therapeutic targets cooperating with MYC.

## sehgalAuerRodsFaggot2023

Sehgal and Sharma review the history, biogenesis, and clinical significance of Auer rods and faggot cells. Auer rods are MPO-positive, peroxidase / PAS / Sudan-black-positive crystalline cytoplasmic inclusions derived from fused azurophilic (primary) granules; bundles of Auer rods in a single cell define a "faggot cell." They are morphologic hallmarks of AML (FAB M1-M6, rare in M0/M7) and are most numerous and characteristic in acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL with t(15;17); PML::RARA), where hypergranular promyelocytes typically display irregularly scattered bundles with a distinctive 250-nm ultrastructural periodicity. In AML their presence generally portends better prognosis (a marker of differentiation), and they upstage MDS (defining MDS-EB2 regardless of blast count). The review also catalogs rarer non-APL contexts (inv(16), t(8;21), t(7;11), mixed-phenotype leukemias) and non-myeloid mimics where Auer-rod-like inclusions occur.

## skalovaMammaryAnalogueSecretory2010

Skálová et al. described 16 salivary gland tumors with morphology and immunoprofile strongly resembling secretory carcinoma of the breast, proposing the new entity "mammary analogue secretory carcinoma" (MASC). Microscopically, tumors showed a lobulated growth pattern with microcystic and glandular spaces filled with abundant eosinophilic / bubbly secretory material that was PAS-, mucicarmine-, MUC1-, MUC4-, mammaglobin-, vimentin-, S-100-, and STAT5a-positive. Critically, the ETV6-NTRK3 t(12;15)(p13;q25) translocation was detected in essentially all analyzable MASC cases and in all three breast SC controls, but was absent in 12 conventional salivary acinic cell carcinomas and other salivary tumor types tested. Most tumors arose in the parotid; clinical course included local recurrences and rare metastatic death. MASC is established as a distinct salivary entity, sharing the defining fusion with breast SC, and biologically separate from acinic cell carcinoma.

WHO Classification of Tumours of Haematopoietic and Lymphoid Tissues, 4th ed. (2008), Swerdlow SH, Campo E, Harris NL, Jaffe ES, Pileri SA, Stein H, Thiele J. ## tognonExpressionETV6NTRK3Gene2002

Tognon et al. identified the t(12;15)(p13;q25) ETV6-NTRK3 (EN) gene fusion — previously known from pediatric mesenchymal tumors (congenital fibrosarcoma, cellular mesoblastic nephroma) — as a recurrent event in human secretory breast carcinoma (SBC), a rare infiltrating ductal carcinoma subtype with well-differentiated glands containing eosinophilic secretions. RT-PCR confirmed EN expression in 12/13 SBC cases and in none of the other ductal carcinomas tested. Retroviral transduction of EN into murine mammary epithelial cells produced transformed cells that formed tumors in nude mice; these tumors retained epithelial differentiation and produced glands. EN is established as a dominantly acting oncogene and primary event in SBC, demonstrating fusion-driven oncogenesis across germ layers. The paper does not discuss salivary MASC, which was characterized later (Skálová et al. 2010).

## tomlinsonGermlineMutationsFH2002

The Multiple Leiomyoma Consortium (Tomlinson et al.) fine-mapped the predisposition locus for an autosomal dominant syndrome of multiple cutaneous and uterine leiomyomata (plus type 2 papillary renal cell carcinoma in some families) to chromosome 1q42.3-q43, identifying germline mutations in FH (fumarate hydratase, a TCA-cycle enzyme). Mutations were predicted to yield absent / truncated protein or alter conserved residues, and FH enzymatic activity was reduced in patient lymphoblastoid cells and very low or absent in their tumors, consistent with classical tumor-suppressor behavior (loss of the wild-type allele in tumors). Biallelic FH mutations cause the recessive fumarate hydratase deficiency, demonstrating that heterozygous vs. homozygous states give distinct clinical phenotypes. The work implicates a housekeeping / mitochondrial enzyme in dominant tumor predisposition. The paper does not address fumarate accumulation as a downstream oncogenic mechanism, nor the inclusion-like nucleolar morphology of HLRCC RCC.
